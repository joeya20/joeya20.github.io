About Me

Profile

I am an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at the University of Calgary, and I am currently completing a 16 month co-op at TC Energy, an Oil and Gas midstream company based in Calgary, Canada. I will finish my co-op and begin the final year of my BSc. in September 2022 and graduate in May 2023. Afterwards, I plan on pursuing an MSc. in Electrical Engineering and I am currently searching for a program in Canada. My potential research interests mainly revolve around digital hardware design, computer architecture and microarchitecture, and hardware design for AI accelerators.

Education

My coursework up to now has been centered around learning the foundations of Electrical and Computer Engineering: digital and analog electronic circuits, writing efficient and maintable code, learning the basics of digital system design and HDLs, and computer organization. In my last year of school, to further develop my design knowledge, I plan on taking courses like ENCM 501 - Principles of Computer Architecture, ENCM 515 - Digital Signal Processors, and ENCM 517 - Computer Arithmetic and Computational Complexity, and if possible, also complete an undergraduate research project under the supervision of a faculty member.

Personal Initiatives

I am continuing to develop my digital design skills using Verilog and I am planning on completing a few projects so keep an eye out on my projects page! I am also learning computer architecture principles using various textbooks listed below to better understand design practices, considerations and tradeoffs. My goal at the end of my internship is to have a solid background in digital design, and computer architecture, and begin applying for MSc. programs.

Textbooks Read

  • Digital Design and Computer Architecture by Harris, and Harris
  • Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach by Hennessy, and Patterson
